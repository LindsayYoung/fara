Data Entry tips

You can copy and paste into all text fields. Don't worry if it looks ugly, extra spaces and line breaks will be removed. For currency fields commas and dollar signs, leading and trailing whitespace will be removed.


Instructions on data fields

Registrant

A registraint is the entity filling out the form. Usually it is a lobbying firm, PR firm or individual. If a registraint is representing itself, fill out the information in the registrant section and the client section.

Lobbyist

This is somone working for the registraint. We identify lobbyists when they make contacts or contributions, you don't have to enter everyone who appears in questions 5(a) and 5(b) of the supplemental.

Try to make sure the lobbyist is not already in the system before adding a new lobbyist to the system. Try searching just the last name to make sure a lobbyist is not already in the system.

Client

This is who the registrant is lobbying on behalf of.

Try not to create new clients if they are already in the system. Always search existing clients before adding a brand new client to the database. Try typing the name without articles like "the" to see if the client is in the system under a slightly different name.

Try to keep the same entities as the same client. For example, the Australian Embassy and the Embassy of Australia are the same. However, different departments of the same institution may be represented separately. The Ecuadorian Embassy and The Ecuadorian Ministry of Tourism would be septate clients. There can only be one client with the same name.

New clients will be added when they appear on questions 8 or 9 or the supplemental or the AB form. When clients are no longer represented, they do not appear as clients, they are transfered to terminated clients.

See explanation of location here.

Terminated Client

When a client is no longer being represented by a firm, mark them as a terminated client. This will happen when a client appears in question 7 of the supplemental or if a client that has been represented by the registrant and is longer reported in questions 8 and 9 of the supplemental.

Only delete clients when they should never have been assigned to the client in the first place- a data entry error or some freak amendment.

Sometimes, you may have to add a client in the database first, to then transfer it to the terminated clients.

Activity and Services

This appears on question 11 of the supplemental. Usually it is a paragraph explaining services. Copy and paste the response. If it refers to contacts, you do not need to double enter the data.

Contact

These are meetings, phone calls, emails, letters or other forms of contact that appear on question 12 of the supplemental. Contacts are often include press, Congress or a governmental departments.

The recipient is the person that the registrant is attempting to contact. Add a a lobbyist if he is mentioned in the contact, otherwise, leave this blank. Don't forget to select what client for the contact. If they don't supply a date, use the supplemental end date.

Recipient

This is the target of the communication, gift or political donation.

  • CRP id: Look this up for members of Congress and Congressional staff
  • Agency: This would be something like Congress, State Department, etc. It could be a News Organization or other entity.
  • Office Detail: This would be the name of the Congressional office or perhaps department.
  • Name: First and last (make sure it is not already in the system)
  • Title: Mr., Sen., Rep., etc.

Payment

Money from the Client to the Registrant. This appears on question 14(a) of the supplemental. It is often added as an addendum so look at the bottom of the form if it is left blank.

  • Fee is used to track money lobbying. {Improve instructions later}

Disbursement

This is money that the registrant spends in service of the client. This can include subcontracts to other lobbying firms etc. This appears in 15(a) in the supplemental.

Contribution

This covers political contributions from PACs and employees of the firm. It can be found on question 15(c) of the supplemental.

Gifts

This covers "things of value" in question 14(c) of the Supplemental.

Processing Info

This tracks the form during the data entry process. When you are done with the form, check "Check when form is finished." This will make the data live on the front end.

Forms that have been processed will say "Form is finished and info is displayed on front end." You can uncheck and click "submit" to hide the data form the front end of the website. Fix the errors and then resubmit.

Location

This section applies to the location of the clients.

  • Location: This is as it appears on the documents
  • Country: This could used for mapping or possibly alerts. This would be the country that the group would appear to be in if we look at map.
  • Region: Use UN regions, find them here. This will be used for alerts.

Stamp Date

This is the day the DOJ Fara office received the form. This is not the start date or end date for the form.


Explanation of forms

Registration Statement is the first filing for the person or organization who lobbies on behalf of a foreign entity.

The Supplemental Statement provides information on what kind of public relations and lobbying a firm engages in on behalf of a foreign government. Supplementals have key information like contacts, payments and contributions. These are filed every 6 months.

Amendments make corrections to past filings.

Short Forms are filed by each person involved in representing a foreign agent. (We don't enter all of these.)

Exhibit A to Registration Statement is filed for each foreign principle and gives an overview of the foreign organization.

Exhibit B to Registration Statement is the agreement between the foreign organization and the firm or person the organization hires. This usually contains a copy of the contract they sign.

Exhibit C contains the Articles of incorporation, bylaws and other documents that pertain to the registrant, the person or company that represents the foreign agent.

Exhibit D outlines fundraising information on behalf of the foreign agent.

Conflict Provision, a letter that is filed to allow a public official to represent a foreign entity. The employer must certify this position is to promote the national interest. These are very rare and might be worth writing about.
